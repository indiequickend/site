Kanchenjunga Valley Retreat sits on a terraced ridge in Barmek, a short drive above Kalimpong town, where the cardamom forests give way to open sky. Four rooms, built from local stone and reclaimed timber, sleep up to fifteen guests across the property.

Mornings start with mist filling the Teesta valley below; by mid-morning it burns off to reveal the river as a silver thread, with Kanchenjunga standing fixed on the horizon above it. Evenings bring the reverse: alpenglow settling on the peak while the valley below goes blue.

This is the first stay under Prime Peak Stays — the one the rest of the chain will be measured against.
